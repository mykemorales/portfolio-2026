McKinsey & Company · VG&M Q3 2025

Process Owner + Record Presence

Overview

Role

Lead/Sole Product Designer & Researcher

Team

1 PM, 1 Tech Lead, 4 Developers

Timeline

4 weeks | 2 sprints

Problem

Post-MVP release of GlobalWorkflow, intake colleagues had no way of knowing whether a colleague had already begun processing a request which led to a spike in duplicate handling across a platform processing on average 130,000 requests per month.

Approach

My initial approach centred on a live record presence indicator component displaying avatars on any Jobsheet being viewed by more than one intake colleague simultaneously. It solved part of the problem: colleagues inside a Jobsheet could now see each other.

But the more I observed how intake colleagues actually worked, the clearer it became that the real collision was happening earlier — at the list view level, before anyone had even opened a request.

Pivot 1

My initial direction explored surfacing record presence directly in the list view using avatars to visually communicate who was already on a request.

Feasibility conversations with the dev lead surfaced a critical gap: avatars only work if you know the faces and/or initials of the colleagues. For a team of 2,500 colleagues, that assumption didn't hold. Providing the string of names in the "Viewers" column proved both more technically viable and significantly clearer in testing.

Pivot 2

Testing revealed a gap we hadn't anticipated, which was knowing a colleague was on a request didn't tell you whether they were handling it or simply reviewing it. Presence without ownership still left room for uncertainty.

Rather than asking intake colleagues to manually assign themselves, I proposed an auto-population model: the moment any change is made on a Jobsheet, that colleague's name is captured as the process owner. Ownership becomes a natural byproduct of doing the work.

Solution

The process owner field became the backbone of the solution by auto-populating with the intake colleague's name the moment they act on a Jobsheet, and surfacing that ownership directly in the list view. This gave colleagues the highest-signal answer to the question that mattered most: not who is looking at this, but who is handling it.

I also designed for the edge cases that would break trust in the system. Colleagues can manually reassign ownership when requests change hands, unassign themselves if they've made any changes to a request in error, and the field clears automatically once a request has been successfully assigned to keep the list view clean and accurate without requiring any extra housekeeping.

Impact

This solution proved to significantly prevent multiple intake colleagues working on the same Jobsheet. Prior to the feature release, duplicate handling of incoming requests was a consistent and escalating complaint among team leads.

Following the introduction of the process owner column, team leads reported that double work had nearly vanished. Across a platform processing an average of 130,000 requests per month, even a conservative 2% duplication rate represents thousands of requests handled twice unnecessarily every month.
